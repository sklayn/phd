Principal Component Analysis

Dataset sediments.sand.red.scaled

This dataset contains 18 individuals and 5 variables, 1 qualitative variable is considered as illustrative.

1. Inertia distribution

The inertia of the first dimensions shows if there are strong relationships between variables and suggests the number of dimensions that should be studied.

The first two dimensions of PCA express 85.46% of the total dataset inertia ; that means that 85.46% of the individuals (or variables) cloud total variability is explained by the plane. This percentage is high and thus the first plane represents an important part of the data variability. This value is greater than the reference value that equals 79.11%, the variability explained by this plane is thus significant (the reference value is the 0.95-quantile of the inertia percentages distribution obtained by simulating 849 data tables of equivalent size on the basis of a normal distribution).

From these observations, it is probably not useful to interpret the next dimensions.

Figure 1 - Decomposition of the total inertia on the components of the PCA

An estimation of the right number of axis to interpret suggests to not interpret the analysis at all. Indeed, the amount of inertia of the first axis is not higher than that obtained by the 0.95-quantile of random distributions (49.43% against 52.39%). This observation suggests that no axis is carrying a real information. As a consequence, the description will stand to these axis.

Figure 2.4 - Qualitative factor map (PCA) The labeled factors are those the best shown on the plane.

The dimension 1 opposes individuals such as 1, 3 and 14 (to the right of the graph, characterized by a strongly positive coordinate on the axis) to individuals such as 7, 12, 8 and 9 (to the left of the graph, characterized by a strongly negative coordinate on the axis).

The group in which the individual 14 stands (characterized by a positive coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variables moisture_content and gravel (variables are sorted from the strongest).

The group in which the individuals 1 and 3 stand (characterized by a positive coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variables silt_clay and TOM (variables are sorted from the strongest).

The group in which the individuals 7, 12, 8 and 9 stand (characterized by a negative coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • low values for the variables gravel, TOM and moisture_content (variables are sorted from the weakest).

Note that the variables Akin and Sozopol are highly correlated with this dimension (respective correlation of 0.91, 0.91). These variables could therefore summarize themselve the dimension 1.
