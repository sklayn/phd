Principal Component Analysis

Dataset sediments.zostera.scaled

This dataset contains 8 individuals and 8 variables, 1 qualitative variable is considered as illustrative.

1. Study of the outliers

The analysis of the graphs does not detect any outlier.

2. Inertia distribution

The inertia of the first dimensions shows if there are strong relationships between variables and suggests the number of dimensions that should be studied.

The first two dimensions of PCA express 81.25% of the total dataset inertia ; that means that 81.25% of the individuals (or variables) cloud total variability is explained by the plane. This percentage is high and thus the first plane represents an important part of the data variability. This value is greater than the reference value that equals 78.52%, the variability explained by this plane is thus significant (the reference value is the 0.95-quantile of the inertia percentages distribution obtained by simulating 878 data tables of equivalent size on the basis of a normal distribution).

From these observations, it is probably not useful to interpret the next dimensions.

Figure 2 - Decomposition of the total inertia on the components of the PCA The first factor is major: it expresses itself 54.34% of the data variability. Note that in such a case, the variability related to the other components might be meaningless, despite of a high percentage.

An estimation of the right number of axis to interpret suggests to not interpret the analysis at all. Indeed, the amount of inertia of the first axis is not higher than that obtained by the 0.95-quantile of random distributions (54.34% against 54.9%). This observation suggests that no axis is carrying a real information. As a consequence, the description will stand to these axis.

Figure 3.4 - Qualitative factor map (PCA) The labeled factors are those the best shown on the plane.

The dimension 1 opposes individuals such as 8 (to the right of the graph, characterized by a strongly positive coordinate on the axis) to individuals such as 7 (to the left of the graph, characterized by a strongly negative coordinate on the axis).

The group in which the individual 8 stands (characterized by a positive coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variables mean_grain_size, sorting and gravel (variables are sorted from the strongest).

The group in which the individual 7 stands (characterized by a negative coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variable sand.
  • low values for the variables moisture_content and silt_clay (variables are sorted from the weakest).

Note that the variables sorting, gravel and Ropotamo are highly correlated with this dimension (respective correlation of 0.95, 0.91, 0.91). These variables could therefore summarize themselve the dimension 1.

The dimension 2 opposes individuals such as 1, 2 and 3 (to the top of the graph, characterized by a strongly positive coordinate on the axis) to individuals such as ** and 7 (to the bottom of the graph, characterized by a strongly negative coordinate on the axis).

The group in which the individuals 1, 2 and 3 stand (characterized by a positive coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variables silt_clay and moisture_content (variables are sorted from the strongest).

The group in which the individual 7 stands (characterized by a negative coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variable sand.
