Principal Component Analysis

Dataset sediments.zostera.red.scaled

This dataset contains 16 individuals and 6 variables, 1 qualitative variable is considered as illustrative.

1. Inertia distribution

The inertia of the first dimensions shows if there are strong relationships between variables and suggests the number of dimensions that should be studied.

The first two dimensions of PCA express 67.04% of the total dataset inertia ; that means that 67.04% of the individuals (or variables) cloud total variability is explained by the plane. The inertia observed on the first plane is smaller than the reference value that equals 73.17%, therefore low in comparison (the reference value is the 0.95-quantile of the inertia percentages distribution obtained by simulating 849 data tables of equivalent size on the basis of a normal distribution). Moreover, the inertia projected on the first dimension is smaller than the reference value 47.83%. The variability expressed by the PCA is thus not significant.

Figure 1 - Decomposition of the total inertia on the components of the PCA

An estimation of the right number of axis to interpret suggests to not interpret the analysis at all. Indeed, the amount of inertia of the first axis is not higher than that obtained by the 0.95-quantile of random distributions (41.45% against 47.83%). This observation suggests that no axis is carrying a real information. As a consequence, the description will stand to these axis.

Figure 2.4 - Qualitative factor map (PCA) The labeled factors are those the best shown on the plane.

The dimension 1 opposes individuals such as 6 and 2 (to the right of the graph, characterized by a strongly positive coordinate on the axis) to individuals such as 14, 13 and 11 (to the left of the graph, characterized by a strongly negative coordinate on the axis).

The group in which the individuals 6 and 2 stand (characterized by a positive coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variables silt_clay and moisture_content (variables are sorted from the strongest).
  • low values for the variable sand.

The group in which the individuals 14, 13 and 11 stand (characterized by a negative coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variable sand.
  • low values for the variables moisture_content and silt_clay (variables are sorted from the weakest).

The dimension 2 particularly distinguishes individuals such as 15 (to the bottom of the graph, characterized by a strongly negative coordinate on the axis).

These individuals form a group sharing : - high values for the variable mean_grain_size.

Note that the variable mean_grain_size is highly correlated with this dimension (correlation of 0.02). This variable could therefore summarize itself the dimension 2.
