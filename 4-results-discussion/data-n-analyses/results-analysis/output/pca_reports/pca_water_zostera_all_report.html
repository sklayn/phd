Principal Component Analysis

Dataset water.zostera.all.scaled

This dataset contains 31 individuals and 9 variables, 1 qualitative variable is considered as illustrative.

1. Inertia distribution

The inertia of the first dimensions shows if there are strong relationships between variables and suggests the number of dimensions that should be studied.

The first two dimensions of PCA express 63.68% of the total dataset inertia ; that means that 63.68% of the individuals (or variables) cloud total variability is explained by the plane. This percentage is relatively high and thus the first plane well represents the data variability. This value is greater than the reference value that equals 49.09%, the variability explained by this plane is thus significant (the reference value is the 0.95-quantile of the inertia percentages distribution obtained by simulating 810 data tables of equivalent size on the basis of a normal distribution).

From these observations, it should be better to also interpret the dimensions greater or equal to the third one.

Figure 1 - Decomposition of the total inertia on the components of the PCA

An estimation of the right number of axis to interpret suggests to restrict the analysis to the description of the first 1 axis. These axis present an amount of inertia greater than those obtained by the 0.95-quantile of random distributions (44.45% against 29.11%). This observation suggests that only this axis is carrying a real information. As a consequence, the description will stand to these axis.

The dimension 1 opposes individuals such as 29 (to the right of the graph, characterized by a strongly positive coordinate on the axis) to individuals such as 2 (to the left of the graph, characterized by a strongly negative coordinate on the axis).

3. Classification

Figure 3 - Ascending Hierarchical Classification of the individuals. The classification made on individuals reveals 5 clusters.

The cluster 1 is made of individuals sharing :

  • low values for the variables Ntotal, chl_a, NH4 and Ninorg (variables are sorted from the weakest).

  • high values for the variable chl_a.
