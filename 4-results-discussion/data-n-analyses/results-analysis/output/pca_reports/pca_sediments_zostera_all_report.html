Principal Component Analysis

Dataset sediments.zostera.all.scaled

This dataset contains 16 individuals and 8 variables, 1 qualitative variable is considered as illustrative.

1. Inertia distribution

The inertia of the first dimensions shows if there are strong relationships between variables and suggests the number of dimensions that should be studied.

The first two dimensions of PCA express 73.71% of the total dataset inertia ; that means that 73.71% of the individuals (or variables) cloud total variability is explained by the plane. This percentage is high and thus the first plane represents an important part of the data variability. This value is greater than the reference value that equals 63.41%, the variability explained by this plane is thus significant (the reference value is the 0.95-quantile of the inertia percentages distribution obtained by simulating 836 data tables of equivalent size on the basis of a normal distribution).

From these observations, it is probably not useful to interpret the next dimensions.

Figure 1 - Decomposition of the total inertia on the components of the PCA

An estimation of the right number of axis to interpret suggests to restrict the analysis to the description of the first 2 axis. These axis present an amount of inertia greater than those obtained by the 0.95-quantile of random distributions (73.71% against 63.41%). This observation suggests that only these axis are carrying a real information. As a consequence, the description will stand to these axis.

Figure 2.4 - Qualitative factor map (PCA) The labeled factors are those the best shown on the plane.

The dimension 1 opposes individuals such as 15 (to the right of the graph, characterized by a strongly positive coordinate on the axis) to individuals such as 141311 (to the left of the graph, characterized by a strongly negative coordinate on the axis).

The group in which the individual 15 stands (characterized by a positive coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variables mean_grain_size, sorting and gravel (variables are sorted from the strongest).

The group in which the individuals 14, 13 and 11 stand (characterized by a negative coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variable sand.
  • low values for the variables moisture_content and silt_clay (variables are sorted from the weakest).

Note that the variable sorting is highly correlated with this dimension (correlation of 0.97). This variable could therefore summarize itself the dimension 1.

The dimension 2 opposes individuals such as 6 and 2 (to the top of the graph, characterized by a strongly positive coordinate on the axis) to individuals such as 14, 13 and 11 (to the bottom of the graph, characterized by a strongly negative coordinate on the axis).

The group in which the individuals 6 and 2 stand (characterized by a positive coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variables silt_clay and moisture_content (variables are sorted from the strongest).
  • low values for the variable sand.

The group in which the individuals 14, 13 and 11 stand (characterized by a negative coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variable sand.
  • low values for the variables moisture_content and silt_clay (variables are sorted from the weakest).
