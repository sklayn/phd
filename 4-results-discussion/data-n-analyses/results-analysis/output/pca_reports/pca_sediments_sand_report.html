Principal Component Analysis

Dataset sediments.sand.scaled

This dataset contains 18 individuals and 8 variables, 1 qualitative variable is considered as illustrative.

1. Study of the outliers

The analysis of the graphs does not detect any outlier.

2. Inertia distribution

The inertia of the first dimensions shows if there are strong relationships between variables and suggests the number of dimensions that should be studied.

The first two dimensions of PCA express 86.56% of the total dataset inertia ; that means that 86.56% of the individuals (or variables) cloud total variability is explained by the plane. This percentage is high and thus the first plane represents an important part of the data variability. This value is strongly greater than the reference value that equals 60.75%, the variability explained by this plane is thus highly significant (the reference value is the 0.95-quantile of the inertia percentages distribution obtained by simulating 842 data tables of equivalent size on the basis of a normal distribution).

From these observations, it is probably not useful to interpret the next dimensions.

Figure 2 - Decomposition of the total inertia on the components of the PCA The first factor is major: it expresses itself 65.46% of the data variability. Note that in such a case, the variability related to the other components might be meaningless, despite of a high percentage.

An estimation of the right number of axis to interpret suggests to restrict the analysis to the description of the first 1 axis. These axis present an amount of inertia greater than those obtained by the 0.95-quantile of random distributions (65.46% against 37.44%). This observation suggests that only this axis is carrying a real information. As a consequence, the description will stand to these axis.

Figure 3.4 - Qualitative factor map (PCA) The labeled factors are those the best shown on the plane.

The dimension 1 opposes individuals such as 2, 1, 14, 13, 15 and 3 (to the right of the graph, characterized by a strongly positive coordinate on the axis) to individuals such as 7, 9 and 8 (to the left of the graph, characterized by a strongly negative coordinate on the axis).

The group in which the individuals 2, 14, 13 and 15 stand (characterized by a positive coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variables gravel, moisture_content, mean_grain_size and sorting (variables are sorted from the strongest).
  • low values for the variable sand.

The group in which the individuals 1 and 3 stand (characterized by a positive coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variables silt_clay, TOM and sorting (variables are sorted from the strongest).

The group in which the individuals 7, 9 and 8 stand (characterized by a negative coordinate on the axis) is sharing :

  • high values for the variable sand.
  • low values for the variables mean_grain_size, sorting, gravel, TOM and moisture_content (variables are sorted from the weakest).

Note that the variables mean_grain_size, sorting, sand and Sozopol are highly correlated with this dimension (respective correlation of 0.93, 0.96, 0.93, 0.91). These variables could therefore summarize themselve the dimension 1.

4. Classification

Figure 4 - Ascending Hierarchical Classification of the individuals. The classification made on individuals reveals 4 clusters.

The cluster 1 is made of individuals such as 7, 8 and 9. This group is characterized by :

  • high values for the variable sand.
  • low values for the variables TOM, moisture_content, sorting, mean_grain_size and gravel (variables are sorted from the weakest).

The cluster 2 is made of individuals such as 16 and 18. This group is characterized by :

  • high values for the variable moisture_content.

The cluster 3 is made of individuals such as 2, 13, 14 and 15. This group is characterized by :

  • high values for the variables gravel, mean_grain_size and sorting (variables are sorted from the strongest).
  • low values for the variable sand.

The cluster 4 is made of individuals such as 1 and 3. This group is characterized by :

  • high values for the variables silt_clay, TOM and sorting (variables are sorted from the strongest).
